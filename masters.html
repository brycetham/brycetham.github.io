Research

Just-In-Time Augmented Learning in the Advanced Manufacturing Context

UCI Institute for Software Research (April 2016 - June 2017)

Faculty Mentor: Dr. Walt Scacchi

Abstract: The use of augmented reality applications can greatly reduce errors and increase productivity in the manufacturing space. Our application, in particular, focuses on the problem of effective training, which refers to issues that are related to when workers are required to learn new or updated skills. In a technique known as just-in-time augmented learning, these “smart” workers can use augmented reality applications to access valuable training resources, which play an important role in informing them of their tasks and responsibilities. This can be done without the need to refer to a secondary laptop computer or tablet, because the resources are loaded directly onto the workers’ augmented reality device, integrating the training process into the workflow and reducing diversion. By using various game-based systems and marker-based augmented reality libraries, we are able to design and develop a software prototype for a head-mounted augmented reality display that demonstrates the feasibility of just-in-time augmented learning for use in the assembly line context.
